Lightfull Studio

Gaming UI for a touch-to-play cybersecurity wall — experienced by hundreds of visitors at a Taipei exhibition.

Year
2024
Client
Lightfull × ZYXEL
Tools
Figma · Illustrator · After Effects
Role
Freelance UI Designer

Overview

For a cybersecurity exhibition at a Taipei trade show, Lightfull Studio (光滿樓) built a life-sized interactive wall where visitors could physically touch and play their way through ZYXEL's protection concepts. I led the full UI, from the arcade-style combat panel to every level screen and HUD.

Brief

Make a firewall feel like a boss fight.

Cybersecurity exhibits, by default, are panels of text on dark backgrounds. The studio's pitch was different — what if visitors could become a security agent for five minutes, and physically punch incoming threats off a wall?

My job was to design every screen that made that metaphor legible to a walk-up audience, in under thirty seconds of attention.

Design

Readable at six feet, believable at one.

Every UI element had to work from two distances — legible to a crowd walking past, and detailed enough to reward the person touching the wall directly. I built a layered visual system: oversized score types and status rings for distance; richer agent portraits, weapon cards, and spectrum readouts for close-up play.

Motion and sound were designed to respond within 80ms of any touch, so the wall felt alive instead of projected.

Outcome

A queue that didn't stop moving.

Across the run of the show, hundreds of visitors — from kids to executives — reached up and played. The line for the wall rarely emptied. For me, the takeaway was craft-level: UI can absolutely live outside a screen, if you design it to fill the room.
